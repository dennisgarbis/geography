Geography Department

Welcome to the Geography Department

Physical Geography

Geography is an interdisciplinary field of study that is well suited for understanding and solving today's complex problems such as global and local wealth inequities, cultural diversity and demographic change, migration, climate change, environmental sustainability etc.

Cultural Geography

Geography is a bridge between the social and natural sciences and today has been greatly enhanced by the growth in geospatial technology tools, such as GPS (Global Positioning Systems), GIS (Geographic Information Systems), and earth imagery from platforms such as satellites and unmanned aerial systems (drones).

GIS, GPS, Remote Sensing

Harper's Geography Department offers general education transfer courses and GIS and drone technology certificates that provide marketable career skills in geospatial technologies (GPS, GIS etc.) and small unmanned aerial systems. All courses help you learn more about our dynamic world.

General Education

Our general education courses provide a comprehensive introduction to geography as an academic discipline. We offer courses in cultural and world regional geography (social science), physical geography (physical science), and a geospatial technology course (social science). The physical geography and geospatial courses are lab classes.

Study Abroad

Geography faculty offer study abroad programs to provide students with global experiences that lead to new perspectives, the ability to navigate different cultures, and increased competitiveness in the job market. Our faculty have led programs to Argentina, Panama, Uganda, and Zimbabwe. Contact Veronica Mormino at vmormino@harpercollege.edu or Mukila Maitha at mmaitha@harpercollege.edu if you are interested in participating in our next program.
